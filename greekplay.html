Ashwith Sai presents a Greek play by Sophocles

Electra and the Author Sophocles

_Enter the_CLYTEMNESTRA

CLYTEMNESTRA

You, if you think me wrong, Bring reasons forth and blame me to my face!

ELECTRA

Thou canst not say this time that I began And brought this on me by some taunting word.

CLYTEMNESTRA

Thou hast my sufferance. Nor would hearing vex, If ever thus you tuned your speech to me.

ELECTRA

Then I will speak. You say you slew him. Where Could there be found confession more depraved, Even though the cause were righteous?

CLYTEMNESTRA

Princess, all hail! To thee and to thy spouse I come with words of gladness from a friend.

ELECTRA

That auspice I accept. But I would first Learn from thee who of men hath sent thee forth?

_Enter the_CHOROUS

CHOROUS

(looking at CLYTEMNESTRA). I see her fuming with fresh wrath: the thought Of justice enters not her bosom now.

CLYTEMNESTRA

What thought of justice should be mine for her, Who at her age can so insult a mother? Will shame withhold her from the wildest deed?

ELECTRA

Not unashamed, assure thee, I stand here, Little as thou mayest deem it. Well I feel My acts untimely and my words unmeet.
